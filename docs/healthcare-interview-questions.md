# Healthcare Professional Interview Guide
## Customer Validation & Market Analysis for PranaScan (PPG-based Vitals App)

**Interview Date:** _______________  
**Interviewee Role/Specialty:** _______________  
**Setting (clinic, hospital, telehealth, etc.):** _______________  

---

## 1. Background & Context

1. Can you walk me through your typical day and the types of patients you see?
2. What are the most common chronic conditions you manage (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, cardiac issues)?
3. How do you currently measure and track patient vitals (heart rate, SpO2, blood pressure, HRV) in your practice?
4. Do you see patients in remote or underserved settings where access to medical-grade devices is limited?

---

## 2. Pain Points & Workflow Gaps

5. What is the biggest friction point when trying to monitor a patient's vitals between clinic visits?
6. How often do patients come in with conditions that could have been caught earlier with continuous or remote monitoring?
7. Do you rely on patient self-reporting for vitals at home? How accurate or reliable is that?
8. Are there specific vitals or biomarkers you wish you could track more continuously or remotely?
9. What are your biggest concerns with the data quality of consumer wearables or phone-based health tools?

---

## 3. Technology Adoption & Willingness to Use

10. Have you or your patients used any smartphone-based health monitoring tools (pulse oximeters apps, ECG apps, etc.)? What was the experience?
11. Would you trust a smartphone camera (PPG/rPPG) to capture heart rate, HRV, or SpO2 for clinical decision-making? Why or why not?
12. What level of clinical validation (studies, FDA clearance, CE mark) would you require before recommending such a tool to patients?
13. What would make you confident enough to integrate a phone-based vitals scan into your triage or follow-up workflow?
14. Are there specific patient populations (elderly, rural, post-discharge) where a low-cost phone-based vitals tool would be most valuable?

---

## 4. Market & Competitive Landscape

15. Are you aware of any existing tools or competitors doing something similar (camera-based vitals)? What do you think of them?
16. What is the typical budget or procurement process for adopting a digital health tool in your organization?
17. Who are the key decision-makers when a new health monitoring tool is introduced (physician, administrator, IT, compliance)?
18. Do you think insurance or payer systems would reimburse for vitals collected via a smartphone app? Under what conditions?
19. Are there regulatory or compliance concerns (HIPAA, data privacy) that would be a barrier to adoption for you or your institution?

---

## 5. Feature Prioritization & Product Direction

20. If you had a tool that could measure heart rate, HRV, SpO2, skin tone-adjusted readings, and vascular age from a phone camera in under 60 seconds — which of those would you find most clinically useful?
21. Would real-time trend tracking (week-over-week changes in vitals) be more valuable than single-session snapshots?
22. Would you want alert notifications if a patient's vitals trend outside a normal range between visits?
23. How important is it that the tool works without internet connectivity (offline-first)?
24. What integrations matter most to you — EHR/EMR systems (Epic, Cerner), messaging apps, or patient portals?
25. Would you want a voice analysis component (detecting respiratory or fatigue patterns) alongside the camera scan?

---

## 6. Business Model & Go-to-Market

26. Would you prefer a per-patient, subscription, or one-time license model for such a tool?
27. Would this be a tool you prescribe/recommend to patients to use on their own devices, or something used inside the clinic?
28. Are there telehealth or RPM (Remote Patient Monitoring) programs at your organization that this could plug into?
29. What would a pilot program look like for you — how many patients, over what duration, and what outcomes would you measure?
30. Would you be open to being an early design partner or beta tester for a product like PranaScan?

---

## 7. Open-Ended / Closing

31. Is there anything about smartphone-based health monitoring that you think most engineers or product teams get wrong?
32. What would be a "killer feature" that would make this a must-have tool in your practice?
33. Is there anyone else in your network — a colleague, patient advocate, or payer representative — you'd suggest I speak with?
34. Any other thoughts, concerns, or advice as I build this product?
